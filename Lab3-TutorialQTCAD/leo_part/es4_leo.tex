\section{File EC.vtu}
\subsection{Analysis of the energy levels}
After importing the file \texttt{DQD-EC.vtu} through the Paraview software,
we can analyze the energy values of the bottom of the conduction band in the different regions of the device.
For our porpuses, we first perform a "slice" of the 3D structure along the plane $z=-2.5$nm: we choose to do it at this 
height because, from the definition of the geometry described in the previous chapter, this is the height where the channel is located between the source and drain condacts. 
In particular, the Silicon layer where the quantum dots are formed extends from $z=0$nm to $z=-10$nm, so the plane at $z=-2.5$nm is well within the channel and allows us to analyze the energy levels in the region of interest.

\begin{figure}[h]
\center
\includegraphics[width=0.7\textwidth]{leo_part/ec_topvieww.png}
\caption{Top view of the conduction band energy levels within the channel, the colorbar is in eV.}
\label{ec_topview}
\end{figure}

As we can see in the figure \ref{ec_topview}, the energy levels, indicated by the colorbar in the bottom right, appear to be higher
in the regions corresponding to the barrier gates while they are lower in the regions below the plunger gates. This is consistent with the 
values of the potential applied to the gates, which are set to 0.5V for the barrier gates and 0.6V for the plunger gates.
This particular configuration can be seen more precisely by analyzing the plot of the conduction band energy along the y direction, which is shown in the figure \ref{plot_ec}.

\begin{figure}[h]
\center
\includegraphics[width=0.7\textwidth]{leo_part/graph_ec.png}
\caption{Plot of the conduction band energy along the y direction at $z=-2.5$nm}
\label{plot_ec}
\end{figure}

From the plot we can clearly observe the formation of barriers and wells in the channel: in correspondence of the barrier gates that separate
the drain and source from the quantum dots, the energy levels are higher representing an energy barrier for the electrons, while below the plunger gates we can appreciate
the formation of two wells where the eventual electron occupying the quantum dot would be confined. Finally, the central barrier gate, where a slightly higher potential is applied in comparison to the other two barrier gates, 
creates a higher energy barrier that has the role of separating the two quantum dots and controlling (in this case suppressing) the tunneling between them.
